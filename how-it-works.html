Process

How PeerReview Hub
Works

From draft submission to polished final work — here's the full peer feedback journey.

Workflow

Four Simple Steps

01
Submit

Upload Your Draft

Drag and drop your essay, research proposal, or case study (PDF, DOCX, or TXT). Your identity remains hidden — only the content is shared. A confirmation message immediately reassures you the file was received.

02
Review

Peers Evaluate Your Work

Fellow students receive your draft anonymously. They complete a structured rubric covering argument, evidence, structure, and language — they cannot skip any section before submitting, ensuring complete feedback.

03
Receive

Get Actionable Feedback

You're notified the moment your review is ready. Read category-by-category scores and written comments designed to help you identify weaknesses and understand exactly what to improve before your final submission.

04
Refine

Revise and Resubmit

Apply the feedback, update your draft, and resubmit for another round if needed. Your revision history dashboard tracks every round so you can see your improvement over time throughout the semester.

Interaction Design

Three Core Interaction Types

Based on Rogers, Sharp & Preece (2023) — the platform is built around three interaction paradigms that make the experience natural and efficient.

📋

Instructing

The dominant interaction type. Students issue discrete instructions — clicking Submit Draft, selecting an assignment, setting a deadline. These instructing interactions match the standardised structure of peer review workflows.

✋

Manipulating

Students drag and drop documents into upload zones, rearrange rubric criteria in customisable templates, and resize annotation panels. Direct manipulation makes the platform feel tactile and familiar.

🔔

Responding

Proactive notifications prompt students to act: "Your draft has been reviewed — view feedback now." Deadline reminders reach reviewers 48 hours before the window closes, keeping the feedback loop moving.

Community

Social Design Principles

Anonymity

Identity-Protected Feedback

Neither submitters nor reviewers can see each other's identities. Research shows anonymity reduces social anxiety and encourages more forthright, honest academic critique — a core design decision on PeerReview Hub.

Social Norms

Community Code of Conduct

A visible code of conduct is embedded directly on the review form. Students can flag abusive or unhelpful reviews. This maintains the integrity of the social environment and sets expectations for respectful engagement.

Collaboration

Optional Group Workspaces

For group projects, shared workspaces let multiple peers review the same document. Optional discussion threads visible only within a defined group extend the social value of review beyond one-way transactions.

Awareness

Social Translucence

Following Erickson & Kellogg (2000), anonymised community statistics show how active others are — review counts, response rates — giving users a sense of presence and shared purpose without exposing identities.
